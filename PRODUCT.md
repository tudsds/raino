# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hardware engineers and makers designing PCBs. They use Raino on a laptop at a workbench or on a phone during commutes and lunch breaks. They are technical, precise, and have limited patience for tool friction. They want to move from idea to Gerber files without being slowed down by the interface.

## Product Purpose

Raino converts fuzzy hardware intent into manufacturing-ready PCB bundles: structured specs, validated BOMs, KiCad projects, and rough cost quotes with confidence bands. Every design decision is bounded by approved architecture templates. Every part traces back to a datasheet or a labeled estimate. The system stops and asks when it hits ambiguity it cannot resolve.

## Brand Personality

**Precise, confident, luminous.** Raino speaks like a senior engineer who has seen every avoidable mistake — calm, direct, never theatrical. The interface rewards expertise and does not perform helpfulness for users who don't need it. Light surfaces, clear hierarchy, motion that earns its presence.

## Anti-references

- Cyberpunk/hacker aesthetics — dark interfaces with neon accents, terminal-style UIs
- SaaS template dashboards — generic card grids with flat color icons, "powered by AI" badges
- Flat Material Design — surface-level elevation with ink ripple effects, roboto defaults
- Decorative glassmorphism used as skin rather than structure
- Monospace as costume for "technical" — fonts are not credentials

## Design Principles

**Constrained depth.** Elevation is purposeful, not decorative. Shadows appear only when state changes — hover, focus, active. Depth conveys hierarchy; it never performs.

**Luminous clarity.** Light backgrounds with strong contrast. The interface is readable under workshop lighting on a laptop screen and under office fluorescent on a phone. Color carries information, not decoration.

**Earned motion.** Motion confirms state, delivers feedback, and reveals hierarchy. It is never ambient and never theatrical. The user's focus is the task, not the animation.

**Honest material.** UI elements look like what they are made of. Glass surfaces are glass — translucent, refractive, depth-layered. Buttons are solid — tactile, responsive. Nothing is pretending to be something else.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance minimum
- Reduced motion respected — no scroll-driven animations, no entrance choreography
- Color is never the sole indicator of state (icons + labels accompany color)
- Touch targets minimum 44×44px on mobile
- Semantic HTML throughout; keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
